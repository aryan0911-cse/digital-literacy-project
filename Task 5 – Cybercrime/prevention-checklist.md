Staying safe in the digital world is just as important as physical safety, especially given how integrated digital tools are into campus life. Here is a checklist of actionable steps you can take today to secure your digital presence.

'Stay Safe Online' Checklist
General Digital Security
Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Set up MFA (using apps like Google Authenticator or hardware keys) on your university portals, email, and social media accounts. This adds a second layer of defense beyond just a password.

Use a Passphrase Manager: Avoid using the same password for your college ID and your personal Netflix. Use a password manager to generate and store complex passphrases (e.g., Mountain-Blue-Pizza-2026!).

Audit App Permissions: Regularly check your smartphone settings to see which apps have access to your microphone, camera, and contacts. Delete any "free" apps that you no longer use.

Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Never log into your bank account or university portal using "Free Campus Wi-Fi" or cafe networks unless you are using a trusted VPN to encrypt your traffic.

Social & Communication Safety
Practice the "Pause" Rule: Before clicking any link in an SMS or WhatsApp message—even if it looks like it’s from your bank or a professor—take 60 seconds to verify the sender’s actual number or email address.

Secure Your Webcam: Use a physical slide cover for your laptop camera. Sophisticated "camfecting" malware can record you without the indicator light ever turning on.

Financial & UPI Safety (India Specific)
The PIN Principle: Remember that a UPI PIN is only required to SEND money, never to receive it. If someone asks you to enter your PIN or scan a QR code to "receive a refund" or "collect a prize," it is a scam.

Verify Merchant Names: When scanning a QR code at a local shop or via a link, always double-check the "Verified Name" that appears on your UPI app (GPay, PhonePe, etc.) before hitting 'Proceed.' If the name looks like a random string of characters, cancel the transaction.

In Case of an Incident
If you or a friend have been a victim of a cybercrime or financial fraud, speed is the most critical factor in recovering funds and securing your identity.

Call the Toll-Free Helpline: Dial 1930 immediately. This is the dedicated national helpline for reporting financial cyber fraud in India.

Pro Tip: Treat your UPI PIN like your ATM PIN—never share it with anyone, not even someone claiming to be a bank official or a campus administrator.
